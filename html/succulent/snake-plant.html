Snake Plant

Saterday, September 21st 2024

By Alex Huang

  • Scientific name：

    Sansevieria trifasciata Prain 'Laurentii'

  • Common name ：

    Saint George's Sword、Mother-In-Law's Tongue

Description

  • A perennial herb in the genus Sansevieria whose parental species is native to eastern and central Africa.This cultivar has distinctive yellow spots on the leaf margin.
  • Rhizomes sturdy and prostrate.
  • Leaves thick and flat, sword-like and upright, blade green with dark green horizontal stripes, margined yellow.
  • Racemes, flowers white and fragrant, flowering in summer.
  • Berries spherical, ripe orange-red.

Cultivation

  • The plant grows in a warm environment with full sun or partial sun and prefers well-drained medium.
  • Resistant to wind, tolerant to pollution and long-term drought.
  • Reproduced from ramets only as cutting propagation cannot pass along the characteristics of yellow margin.

Application

  • Featuring with a unique leaf shape and bright leaf colors, it’s suitably grown as a garden groundcover, hedge or in an indoor pot.
  • Indigenous Africans used the leaf fibers to make bowstrings.
  • The leaf cuttings can be used as flower arranging material.
