Bird's-nest Fern

Saterday, September 21st 2024

By Alex Huang

  • Scientific name：

    Asplenium Antiquum

  • Common name ：

    Nest Fern、Crown's Nest Fern

Description

  • A perennial epiphytic herb of the genus Asplenium, distributed from Africa, Polynesia, Australia, tropical Asia, and Southeast Asia.
  • Rhizomes short and stout; fronds produced from rhizomes in annular arrangement and spreading outward to create a massive leaf nest.
  • Fronds lanceolate, apex acuminate, entire and slightly wavy; midribs prominently protruding on the underside; frond green on the upper surface.
  • Petiole short and fleshy, black, with black-brown linear scales at the base; sori arranged linearly between the midrib and the margin; spreading from the midrib and along the lateral veins.

Cultivation

  • The plants grow slowly in winter, but develop rapidly in summer and autumn, preferable temperature between 20-30°C.
  • Keep out of direct sunlight while still providing bright, indirect light.
  • Propagated by spores.

Application

  • Can be used as shade groundcover, planted in pots indoors, in small pots or as epiphyte.
