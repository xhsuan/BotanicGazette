Philodendron Verrucosum

Sunday, October 27st 2024

By Lisa Wu

  • Scientific name：

    Philodendron Verrucosum

  • Common name ：

    Velvet-leaf Philodendron, Ecuador Philodendron

Description

  • It's Leaf is velvety, soft, and smooth to the touch.
  • Heart-shaped leaves with pointed tips. Deep green with prominent, brightly colored veins, often in shades of red or gold.
  • Mature leaves can grow up to 30–45 cm in length, depending on the growing conditions.

Cultivation

  • Only water when it’s dry. Overwatering or underwatering can cause drooping leaves. Avoid prolonged wetness to prevent root rot. In winter, reduce watering.
  • Philodendrons prefer indirect light. Insufficient light may cause leggy growth and larger gaps between leaves, while excessive light can yellow the leaves.
  • Maintaining a relative humidity of 55–60% is ideal for their growth.
  • Feed monthly with a balanced liquid fertilizer during spring and summer. In autumn and winter, reduce feeding to once every 6–8 weeks.

Application

  • With its stunning, velvety heart-shaped leaves and vibrant vein patterns, it's widely used as an ornamental plant in homes, offices, and interior design projects.
  • It's often used by horticulturists in hybridization to develop new Philodendron varieties. Its unique foliage and attractive growth habit make it a valuable parent plant for creating hybrids with striking features.
