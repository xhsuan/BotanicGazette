Taiwan Rhododendron

Saterday, September 21st 2024

By Emily Lin

  • Scientific name：

    Rhododendron formosanum Hemsl

  • Common name ：

    Red-Stripe Rhododendron

Description

  • An evergreen tree in the genus Rhododendron, endemic to Taiwan.
  • The tree can reach 20m in height, and the trunk is often curved; bark thin, reddish-brown, not easily exfoliated; branchlets long and thick, erect or obliquely rising.
  • Leaves tufted at the branchlet tips, lanceolate to oblanceolate or oblong, leathery, margin entire or recurved inward.
  • Umbel, 7-10 flowers, terminal or nearly terminal; corollas broadly campanulate in shape, white, sometimes tinged red or brown flecks on the throat.
  • Capsules oval with brown fluff.

Cultivation

  • The plant prefers full sun and cold conditions and acidic soil; wind-resistant and drought-tolerant.
  • Propagated by seeds or cuttings.

Application

  • Prized for the lovely flowers and unique shape, the plant is suitable to be a garden landscape tree or used for environmental education and ecological restoration in the middle and high altitude mountain area.
